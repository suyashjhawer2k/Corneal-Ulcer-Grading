What is Corneal Ulcer

A corneal ulcer is an open sore on your cornea. Infection is the leading cause. Symptoms include a red, watery and bloodshot eye; severe eye pain and pus or other eye discharge. A corneal ulcer can lead to vision loss and blindness. It’s considered a medical emergency. How is a corneal ulcer diagnosed? Your eye care provider will: Perform an examination with a slit lamp microscope. The slit lamp focuses a narrow “slit” of light onto the eye. A slit lamp exam is a normal part of an eye exam. Your provider may apply a fluorescein dye to your eye. This yellow dye highlights any damage to your cornea. Take a sample of the infected tissue. The results will show the type of infection and guide medication choice for treatment.
